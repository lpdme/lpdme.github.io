By means of this collaborative platform, we provide archives of PDF files optimized for home-printing (posters & booklets) which can be downloaded free of charge, filed and reconstructed by the user. Standard A4 format as a brick, in the words of the designer Victor Papanek, «the result would be a mass produced object, in the meaning of the Bauhaus, but where each piece would be involved with a differentiation potential» [note: «… in the framework of the Modern Movement, creators rejected the emotional signifiance of local traditions or materials and worked with the (naïve) conviction that the form, content or interest of their works would be universal», in Design for the Real World, Human Ecology and Social Change, 1970].

We propose quotations, articles & essays, notably concerning the history of graphic design and typography but also literature or sociology. Urban epigraphy (mural inscriptions, signage systems, etc.) envisaged from the viewpoint of the overall phenomenon of metropolization & suburbanization [note: this world-wide phenomenon is characterized by the dual simultaneous movement of concentration towards the different megacities and a spatial extension & dissemination of their urban areas], constitutes one of our preferential spheres of exploration. Our productions are proposals for reaction techniques to our daily visual environment, graphic responses in the public domain, i.e. providing the tools, the manuals for action with economy of means, like for instance street lettering models.

Our manifesto-name derives from a typical French expression [note: «partisan du moindre effort» describes a person doing ‹as less effort as possible›, i.e. a lazy behaviour] that has here to be understood as a quest for simplicity applied at two levels: in design, on the one hand, in message receiving, on the other hand. The ease of perception, the visibility & legibility of the information conveyed is of prime importance. The «least resistance» action line in our name is thus paired with a «least effect» preference («le moindre effet» in French).
